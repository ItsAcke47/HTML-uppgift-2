The Way of Kings

Exploring the First Three Books of Brandon Sanderson's Stormlight Archive Brandon Sanderson's Stormlight Archive is a cornerstone of modern epic fantasy. Known for its intricate world-building, deep characters, and a magic system tied to nature and oaths, the series has captivated millions of readers. While ten books are planned, Sanderson has released the first five so far, with the first three—The Way of Kings, Words of Radiance, and Oathbringer—forming the foundation of this grand tale. Let’s dive into the highlights of each of these books and what makes them stand out. 1. The Way of Kings: A World Unveiled Released in 2010, The Way of Kings introduces us to the storm-swept world of Roshar, where magical storms known as "highstorms" regularly ravage the landscape. At the center of the story is Kaladin, a former soldier turned slave who finds himself trapped in one of the deadliest warzones in Roshar: the Shattered Plains. As Kaladin grapples with despair and a desire to protect those around him, he embarks on a journey toward discovering his latent powers as a Windrunner—one of the legendary Knights Radiant. Meanwhile, we also follow Shallan Davar, a young scholar with a dark secret, as she tries to save her family from ruin while studying under the enigmatic Jasnah Kholin. Shallan's journey is one of intellectual discovery as she learns the secrets of the ancient world and her place within it. The third central figure is Dalinar Kholin, a high prince haunted by visions from the past, urging him to unite the warring factions of Roshar. Dalinar's moral integrity and struggle with leadership form the emotional core of the novel.

Words of Radiance

The second installment, Words of Radiance (2014), picks up immediately after the events of The Way of Kings and focuses heavily on Shallan and her developing abilities as a Lightweaver—another Knight Radiant order. Shallan’s journey toward mastering her powers and uncovering the hidden history of the Knights Radiant propels much of the plot, and her intelligence and wit make her a fan favorite. Kaladin's role deepens as well. Now a captain in Dalinar's guard, he must learn to balance his new responsibilities while dealing with his burgeoning powers. His journey continues to focus on leadership and duty, but this time against a backdrop of political tensions and assassination plots. Dalinar, on the other hand, faces the challenge of uniting the fractured kingdom of Alethkar and preparing for the looming threat of the Parshendi, the enemy race whose nature and origins become central to the larger narrative. The discovery of the Parshendi's connection to Roshar's mysterious past adds a layer of moral complexity to the conflict, as Sanderson begins to blur the lines between "good" and "evil."

Oathbringer

Released in 2017, Oathbringer shifts much of the focus to Dalinar Kholin as he grapples with his dark past, seeking redemption. The book explores Dalinar's transformation from a ruthless warlord into a man striving for peace and unity. Through a series of flashbacks, readers learn about his violent history and the traumatic events that led him to take on the mantle of leadership. These revelations add immense depth to his character and elevate his internal conflict. Meanwhile, Kaladin faces new challenges, struggling with the knowledge that not all Parshendi are enemies and that a devastating war might be on the horizon. His story arc deals with themes of prejudice, responsibility, and understanding others beyond the surface. Shallan, still dealing with her fragmented sense of self, faces even more pressure to embrace her Lightweaver abilities as tensions rise between different factions. Her arc in Oathbringer centers around her struggles with mental health, identity, and the complexities of her relationships with those around her, particularly with Adolin and Kaladin. At the heart of Oathbringer is the looming Everstorm, a destructive force that heralds the return of the Voidbringers, ancient enemies of humanity. As Dalinar works to unite the world against this existential threat, he is forced to confront both external and internal enemies.
